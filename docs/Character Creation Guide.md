# Character Creation Guide

**Create your character and start playing!**

A character consists of a few aspects:

- **Stats** are used to perform tests
- **Aptitudes** give your character special abilities based on their training, or innate abilities
- **Keywords** are narrative descriptors that you can use to justify advantage
- **Inventory Slots** are where you stow your gear and equip spells

Creating a character in Rath RPG is as fast and easy as you want it to be. If you're new to RPGs, Just follow the steps below.

---

## Aptitudes and Species

In Rath RPG, Aptitudes are special abilities that help differentiate and define who and what your character is, what they're capable of, and what their specialties are. These work in conjunction with **Keywords** to define your character.

Your aptitude choices at creation determine your species:

- If you take 2 **Skill Aptitudes**, your character is **Human** (or close enough)
	- Humans are versatile, adaptable learners
- If you take at least 1 **Inherent Aptitude**, your character is a **Demihuman**
	- Demihumans have innate capabilities or abilities

Think of **Aptitude** choices as "building a class"

**Skill Aptitudes** are learned abilities (combat techniques, magic, thievery, survival).

**Inherent Aptitudes** are innate traits (darkvision, natural weapons, wings, scales).

## Step 2: Keywords

Choose **2-3 keywords**:

1. **Species** (Required): Human, Dwarf, Elf, Orc, Halfling, Construct, etc.
2. **Role** (Required): Fighter, Thief, Wizard, Ranger, Barbarian, etc.
3. **Background** (Optional): Add now or during play (Noble, Outlaw, Sailor, etc.)

**Using Keywords:** Justify advantage on relevant tests ("As a Ranger, I have advantage tracking this beast"). They also help define your character narratively. 

---

## Step 3: Assign Stats

Choose ONE method:

### Method 1: Plan Ahead
Distribute these values: **3, 2, 2, 1, 1, 0**

Place in any order across your six stats.

### Method 2: Roll the Bones
Roll **3d6 for each stat in order** (STR, DEX, INT, WIS, CON, CHA).

The **lowest die** = that stat's value.

When done, you may **swap any two stats**.

### The Six Stats

| Stat | Used For |
|---|---|
| **STR** | Melee attacks, lifting, breaking, physical power |
| **DEX** | Ranged attacks, dodging, climbing, sneaking |
| **INT** | Magic, resisting magic, recalling lore, crafting |
| **WIS** | Perception, tracking, navigation, finding secrets |
| **CON** | Resisting poison/sickness/environment, added to healing |
| **CHA** | Persuasion, deception, intimidation, charm |

---

## Step 4: Calculate Derived Stats

| Stat | Formula |
|---|---|
| **HP** | 10 + STR (+ aptitude bonuses) |
| **AC** | 10 + DEX + armor bonus |
| **Inventory Slots** | 10 + CON |

## Step 5: Equipment

### Option A: Choose a Gear Pack

Pick one pack. All packs include **UD8 Rations** and **2d6+5 copper pieces**.

| Pack | Contents |
|---|---|
| **Combat** | Medium armor (+2 AC, 3 slots), standard weapon (d8, 2 slots), shield (+1 AC, 1 slot), torches |
| **Scout** | Light armor (+1 AC, 2 slots), bow (d6, 2 slots), dagger (d6, 1 slot), rope 50ft, lantern |
| **Caster** | Staff (d6, 1 slot), candles, blank book, tinderbox, lantern |
| **Specialist** | Light armor (+1 AC, 2 slots), 2 daggers (d6, 1 slot each), lockpicks, rope 50ft, grappling hook |
